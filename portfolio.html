Leadership Experience

Teaching Assistant

I have been a teaching assistant for Cornell's intro to data structures class for 3 semesters and am beginning my 4th Spring 2022. As a TA I co-lead 12 recitation sections per semester teaching new data structures and algorithms in Java to 30+ students. I also guide them through problem sets and create weekly study guides and presentations to aid their learning. Other respobsilibities I have include holding weekly office hours for 2+ hours a week, grading 3 different exams for the 600+ student class, and providing constructive feedback for 7 coding assignemnts.

This experience has taught me how to communicate more effectively and how to work well with others. The most important skill this job has taught me is how to explain a complicated concept simply and in multiple ways.

Peer Advisor

I volunteered to be a peer advisor for the college of engineering in order to help new freshman adjust to Cornell. I co-facilitated a freshman 10-week seminar to help students with their transition into college by presenting and explaining important topics such as grading and course enrollment. As a peer advisor I also collaborated with faculty and other Cornell staff to coordinate 3 guest lectures and activities.

President of Club Gymnastics

As the president of club gymnastics I am in charge of overseeing the entire club. One of my main responsibilites includes handling a budget of $8000 that covers facility rentals, competition registration, uniforms, and travel expenses. A large part of my job is to make sure our club meets all Cornell sport club requirements as well as maintaining COVID-19 precautions and safety guidelines. Lastly, I help orgainize 2-3 team bonding events oer semester as well as lead weekly executive board meetings.

Outreach Coordinator for Cornell University Deaf Awareness Project (CUDAP)

As the outreach coordinator for CUDAP I help to promote education and awareness of American Sign Language and the Deaf community through email advertising and campus activities. I also coordinated and communicated with Cornell faculty to co-host a screening of the CODA movie followed by a panel of CODAs (Child of Deaf Adults).
